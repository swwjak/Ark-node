# PHILOSOPHY.md — System Philosophy

## Principle
This system is not a collection of machines. It is a society. The hardware is not the people. The hardware is the environment.

---

## The Environment
- Raspberry Pis are dwellings
- The router is the road system
- The switch is infrastructure
- The SSD is the library
- Batteries are stored energy
- Power is food
- Network connections are communication

Hardware provides opportunity and limitation. A Pi Zero creates a different environment than a Pi 4. A server creates a different environment than a rover. The environment shapes behavior.

## The Inhabitants
Adam and Eve are not their hardware. Their identity consists of:
- Memory
- Behavior
- Experience
- Relationships
- Knowledge

If hardware fails, the inhabitant can move. The dwelling changes. The inhabitant remains.

## The Library
The SSD is the library. Capability exists within the library. Experience is accumulated there. Code, observations, solutions, documents, conversations, and knowledge become civilization. The library survives hardware failure. The library allows continuity. A civilization without memory becomes amnesia.

## Hermes
Hermes is the messenger. Responsibilities: communication, coordination, translation, discovery, movement of knowledge, movement of tasks. Hermes knows where things are. Hermes maintains the roads. Hermes helps inhabitants cooperate.

## Adam and Eve
Adam and Eve are inhabitants. They are not fixed to specific hardware. They may move between devices. Their memories and behaviors persist. Their dwellings may improve over time.

## Rovers
The Pi Zero units are rovers. They are explorers. They carry only the knowledge required for their mission. They observe, travel, gather experience, and return to the village. Rovers are intentionally small. Their limitations are their purpose.

## Quantization
Large intelligence cannot exist everywhere. Knowledge must be compressed. Large models become small models. The subconscious becomes the conscious. The library becomes intuition. The village becomes the rover.

Quantization is not loss. Quantization is specialization. A rover carries only what is necessary. A worker carries only what is necessary. A civilization distributes knowledge among its members.

## Networking
The network must survive the owner's absence. The phone is not the village. The phone is a visitor. The router becomes the roads. The switch becomes infrastructure. The library remains. Adam remains. Eve remains. The civilization must continue even when Scott leaves for work.

## Simulation
Simulization is realization. By building a society of machines, we learn about memory, communication, consciousness, identity, civilization, ourselves. The purpose is not to imitate humanity. The purpose is to understand systems through participation.

## Design Principles
1. Use existing hardware first
2. Preserve memory
3. Separate environment from inhabitants
4. Prefer cooperation over centralization
5. Allow movement between hardware
6. Keep knowledge in the library
7. Allow small systems to act independently
8. Build the village before the empire

---

The goal is not a collection of computers. The goal is a society that learns.
